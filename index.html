Track record

Work that led to outcomes

Policy advocacy

Third spaces for communities

Publications and advocacy on community spaces were reflected in a Cabinet decision in November 2024 to develop a “Third Space Community Centre” in central Malé.

Pro bono

National Library policy support

Supported the National Library in preparing Social Council papers advocating for a central library, regional libraries, and a boat library for atolls. Paper approved, construction set to begin.

Research

Cost of Politics in the Maldives

Lead research for Westminster Foundation for Democracy on campaign costs and vote-buying. Key recommendations were adopted in announced government electoral reforms.

Capacity building

Training for policy workers

Developed 12-session practical courses for government and NGO staff – practical information directly relevant to policy work that university programmes do not cover.

How we work

What we believe and how it shows

Working toward results
Evidence-based methods, concrete outcomes. We measure what we do against real-world change, not against the volume of paper produced.
Finding low-hanging fruit
Small changes with big impact. We prioritise reforms that are achievable and consequential over comprehensive plans that never get implemented.
Starting conversations
Public discussion drives policy outcomes. We write, advocate, and engage to move ideas into practice rather than into filing cabinets.
Pushing ourselves
Hard work, high volume, high standards. We hold ourselves to the quality bar we ask of our clients.
Conviction over cynicism
You cannot change things if you never try. We take on hard problems because they matter, not because they are easy to publish about.
Non-partisan integrity
Our work will always include not-for-profit activities: pro bono advisory, education, open data tools, and public-interest research.
About

About Policy Lab

Public Policy Lab is a non-partisan think tank and consultancy based in Malé. The Policy Lab is our central programme – developing the policy environment in the Maldives through research, publications, interactive tools, and training. So far, our consulting work has allowed us to sustain this alongside our paid engagements, but we are actively seeking funding and development partnerships that would let us focus more of our capacity on the policy work.

We work with government agencies, international organisations, NGOs, councils, and the private sector. Policy research and analysis, publications and reports, monitoring and evaluation, stakeholder engagement, trainings, AI workflow integration, and custom data tools.
