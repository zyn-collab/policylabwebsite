Westminster Foundation for Democracy · Sept 2024 – Feb 2025

Cost of Politics in the Maldives

PPL led the research and principal authorship of this study, commissioned by WFD and funded by the British High Commission under the Integrated Security Fund. The project examined how money shapes electoral politics in the Maldives – not only during campaign periods but in the lead-up to and aftermath of elections.

The research drew on 22 in-depth interviews with candidates and experts, 6 written questionnaires, and a citizen focus group – carefully sampled across both major parties, independents, Malé and regional constituencies, male and female candidates, youth and experienced campaigners, winners and losers. The study documented campaign economics in granular detail: average budgets of MVR 2–5 million per race, 75–90% allocated to vote-buying, a going rate of approximately MVR 5,000 per vote, primary elections costing MVR 200,000–4 million with no party financial support, and 40–100 SOE positions promised per constituency as campaign currency. Findings were validated in a session with 12 representatives from civil society, government, party leadership, and election bodies. The report was co-authored with a team from Align Consultancy LLP, which included a former Attorney General and former Deputy Attorney General.

After publication, both major parties incorporated aspects of the recommendations into public statements. The government announced plans for electoral reforms matching key recommendations – including combining presidential and parliamentary elections and introducing ranked-choice voting – and the President proposed holding a public referendum on these reforms.

UNICEF Maldives · Since October 2025

Child and Family Well-Being Project

PPL serves as lead consultant on this multi-component 15-month national initiative addressing the systemic drivers of family instability in the Maldives. The project spans six integrated components that would typically each be scoped as a separate consultancy: vulnerability mapping research, prenuptial agreement templates, a digital premarital training programme, a national mediator training programme, Family Court data standardisation, and child support enforcement.

The vulnerability mapping centred original qualitative research with 19 in-depth interviews (1.5–3 hours each, conducted in Dhivehi in private settings) and two focus groups with 70+ participants, mapping vulnerabilities across four life stages. The draft publication runs approximately 90,000 words with around 50 recommendations. PPL developed standardised prenuptial agreement templates covering six areas of financial and family protection, built a six-course premarital training programme comprising approximately 200 content slides, 37 interactive checkpoints, and 26 video concepts – and built a functional working demo of the digital delivery platform. The mediator training component targets 250+ mediators across all 19 atolls through a 7-day Training of Trainers programme with cascading 5-day atoll-level trainings. The project also includes a situation analysis and redesign of the Family Court's data systems.

Ministry of Social & Family Development · Completing July 2026

KnowHow: knowledge management system

PPL is developing a comprehensive, searchable online knowledge management system for MSFD called KnowHow, housing 51 chapters of research, reference materials, institutional tools, training content, templates, workplans, and archival records. The system was built to solve a specific problem: knowledge relevant to social policy was scattered across agencies, servers, and individual staff members' memories, and when staff turned over, the knowledge they carried left with them.

The core is a 15-part social policy encyclopedia covering the full landscape of Maldivian social policy. The four domains the Ministry is directly responsible for run to substantial depth: Child Protection and Child Rights (111,589 words across 124 sections), Gender, GBV, and Women's Empowerment (109,374 words, 120 sections), Disability and Inclusive Social Protection (55,314 words, 68 sections), and Elderly Care and Population Ageing (65,973 words, 115 sections). Each domain is paired with sector training materials designed to be adapted into full interactive courses.

Beyond the encyclopedia, the system houses a cross-domain literature review, statistics compendium, graphs and charts library, publications library, capacity assessment, island-level service provider mapping across all atolls, CSO and NGO directories, expert roster, legislation timeline, treaty obligations mapping (CRC, CEDAW, CRPD), indexed Dhivehi-language news archives, standardised document templates, and the Ministry's 2025–2028 project portfolio and annual workplan.

For the community

Public-interest work we do for free

Alongside our project work, we run a set of free community services and resources. Our advocacy for third spaces and community cultural programming was reflected in a Cabinet decision in November 2024 to develop a "Third Space Community Centre" in central Malé.

Events & venue

  • Community events – Movie screenings, trivia nights, poetry readings, and other low-key cultural events open to the public, working toward a "365 Arts City" where on any given night anyone can go out and find something to do.
  • Free venue for artists – Our outdoor terrace space in central Malé is available free for small artists and at heavily discounted rates for NGOs. The main terrace is roughly 400 sq ft with seating, a swing, a projection wall with portable projector and speakers, fairy lights, plants, and picnic mats. A further 500 sq ft upstairs space is accessible by an outdoor staircase – roughly 900 sq ft total, ready for screenings, readings, exhibitions, or small gatherings.
  • Chapbook publishing – We print and publish copies of local writers' and poets' chapbooks for free, distributed at our community events.

The terrace and upstairs space at our office in central Malé.

Free services

  • CV screening and advice – Free CV and cover letter reviews with practical advice on improving applications, available to anyone who signs up online. We help people put their best foot forward when applying for jobs.
  • Internships – Research internships for young professionals in the policy sector, with guided research projects, reading plans, and practical training. Four interns have gained research and professional experience with us so far.
  • Training courses – 12-session practical seminars for government and NGO staff, covering what university programmes do not teach about working in policy. Online versions coming soon.
  • Transport advocacy – A voluntary pledge for SMEs to reimburse all employee public transport and rideshare expenses – which began with our own implementation for our team – alongside research documenting how much of Malé's streets are consumed by parked vehicles.

Free resources

  • Data visualization gallery – Screenshottable charts and maps built from census, survey, and administrative data. Designed for students, researchers, academics, and journalists to use in reports, presentations, and papers. Free with attribution.
  • NutritionMV – Open nutrition profiles for Maldivian food, starting with hedhikaa (shorteats). The first public collection of its kind.
  • Service directories & timelines – Every FCSC, health facility, police station, court, and CSO mapped across 20 atolls, plus an interactive timeline of social policy legislation from 1927 to 2025.
  • Island Data Explorer – Demographics, infrastructure, transport, elections, and development projects for 21 islands. Built with Australian government funding, with nationwide expansion pending additional support.
  • All publications – Every policy brief, essay, and analysis we publish is free and open access.
