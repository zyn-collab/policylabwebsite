# Urbanism Page – Scratchpad

## Core Aesthetic (from merch design)
- Color palette: muted teal (#6B9E8D), coral (#CC7B6F), light coral (#D9A49D), sage grey (#8A9A8E)
- "cities built for human beings" – multi-color, mixed weights (built/human bold, others regular)
- Uppercase ticker: "MORE BUS STOPS · MORE BUS ROUTES · MORE CITY TREES..." (animated scroll)
- Clean white/off-white background, sophisticated and warm not corporate
- Typography: Source Serif 4 headings, DM Sans body

## Content Pieces
1. **Bus routes** (FLAGSHIP) – 5 proposed changes, interactive maps exist, BusRoutes_Article.html
2. **Street parking** – 43.5% road width blocked, 133 measurements, 27 streets, field research 2024
3. **Greenery** – Male' 12-16% vs 40% threshold, 1.4°C per 10pp tree cover
4. **Third spaces** – Policy framework, led to Cabinet decision Nov 2024
5. **Community events** – Film screenings + poetry, 6-16 people per event, pilot
6. **Venue network** – Distributed mini-venues, existing businesses host during off-peak
7. **Elderly shuttle** – Transport as binding constraint, working with Israhvehinge Naadhee
8. **Design language** – Medhuziyaaraiy/Hukuru Miskiyy, draft for ADB
9. **Old City** – 35% streets already vehicle-free, pedestrianised district concept

## Key Bus Route Arguments (user emphasis)
- Current system = airport shuttle, not city bus (ferry-era mindset)
- MVR 30 taxi + bus ticket > MVR 45 direct taxi → people skip bus
- 16 bus passengers = road space of 1 motorcycle
- Routes through Majeedhee Magu → cinema, sports, shopping accessible to Hulhumale
- Male' residents → Hulhumale beaches/parks cheaply and vice versa
- Government employees at Velaanaage etc get doorstep service
- Majeedhee Magu stops = employees at businesses also served (not just govt workers)
- Young people especially benefit – less costly, don't need motorcycle
- Less parking clutter, congestion, pollution
- Mini buses within Male' require additional transfers vs direct Hulhumale bus
- Culture shift: make bus use normal

## Design Principles (from guidelines + user instructions)
- Design serves content, not templates
- No template-filling (don't pull stats just to fill slots)
- Every visual element communicates something
- Scroll-driven, flowing, gallery of ideas
- Click to explore, not all text at once
- No emoji, no marketing-speak
- Intentional: every choice should reinforce the story and image
- Coherent with the message – urbanist, thoughtful, not slapped together
- " – " (en dash with spaces), sentence case
