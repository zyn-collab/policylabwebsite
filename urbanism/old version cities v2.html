Malé is one of the densest cities on Earth. We've been working on what it would take to make it more liveable – from bus routes that actually reach workplaces to community spaces where people can spend time without spending money. Below is our work across transport, public space, street design, and city identity. Click through to read, explore interactive maps, or see our research data.

Space usage by street parking

Third spaces & cultural life

Places to be that aren't home or work – and things to do when you get there.

🏛️
Policy impact achieved
Third spaces, vibrant cities
The policy framework that led to a Cabinet decision on youth third spaces in November 2024. Covers the full taxonomy – from public libraries down to a café's back room hosting weekly trivia.
Cabinet decision achieved →

Pilot completed
Community cultural programming
Free film screenings and poetry nights on off-peak evenings. We tested whether small, frequent, low-promotion events work. People showed up. Now scaling across multiple venues.
Scaling in progress →

Policy brief
A network of small venues for arts and culture
Distributed mini-venues across the city where 15 people watching a film counts as a success. Existing businesses provide space during off-peak hours. A city-wide calendar means something is always on.

Streets, greenery & heat

What happens when you reclaim road space from machines that sit idle 95% of the day.

12–16%
greenery cover in Malé (40% needed for cooling)
Research
Greenery estimation in Malé City
Satellite analysis of green cover. Malé: 12-16%. Northern Hulhumalé Phase 1: 30-45%, the only area near the 40% threshold. Every 10pp of tree cover reduces air temperature by ~1.4°C.

City identity & design

What Malé could look like if it drew on its own history and environment.

🕌
Draft · prepared for ADB
A Maldivian urban design language
Design guidelines rooted in the Medhuziyaaraiy/Hukuru Miskiyy complex – white and blue palettes, Islamic geometric patterns, coconut leaf motifs, weathered stone. What government buildings could look like if they had principles.
Draft for ADB →
🗺️
Concept paper
Old City and Capital District
Designating Malé's historical and government core as a pedestrianised "Old City" with a Capital Plaza, shaded walkways, and direct bus connectivity. 35% of streets in the area are already vehicle-free.
