institutional reform

Government capacity constraints are rarely about individual competence and frequently about systems that lose knowledge between administrations, duplicate work across departments, fragment digital services across hundreds of disconnected portals, and leave staff without the tools or documentation to do their jobs. These briefs diagnose specific institutional dysfunctions and propose interventions that build cumulative capability rather than one-off fixes.

Diagnosis · systems analysis
Organizational efficiency
How information infrastructure gaps cause policy failure across government. Knowledge that exists in one department is invisible to another; work done under one administration is repeated under the next; staff spend hours producing documents that could be templated. The diagnosis that frames the interventions in every other brief on this page.

This brief connects to everything else here: the knowledge repository addresses the storage problem, institutional memory addresses the transition problem, digitalization addresses the access problem, the prompter addresses the production problem, and pro bono support addresses the capacity problem. Each intervention makes the others more effective.
Knowledge infrastructure
Knowledge management repository
A centralised searchable digital repository of policy documents, research, news archives, and literature reviews. Prevents the cycle where every new team spends months doing background research that previous teams already completed, because that work was stored in email inboxes and personal drives.

Continuity · documentation
Institutional memory preservation
Comprehensive documentation of programmes and policies across all departments so that institutional knowledge survives staff turnover and political transitions. What was tried, what worked, what didn't, and why – recorded in a form that the next team can actually find and use.

Capacity · implementation
Pro bono capacity support
Ongoing pro bono technical support relationships that fill specific capacity gaps in public sector agencies. Case study: how sustained technical support helped the National Library secure MVR 270 million in investments it could not have pursued without external expertise.

AI tools · templates
Standard prompter
Form-based AI prompting templates that encode institutional best practices for common government documents – cabinet papers, UN reporting formats, policy briefs, analytical reports. Quality becomes a function of the template design rather than individual skill, making strong outputs accessible to any staff member.
