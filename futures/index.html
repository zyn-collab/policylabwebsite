strategic foresight

The Maldives faces convergent pressures over the next 15 years – demographic aging, climate acceleration, geopolitical realignment, AI disruption, health system strain – and the current planning horizon is too short to prepare for any of them. These briefs map what's coming, what the current policy consensus looks like, what other countries do, and what building genuine resilience requires.

Policy challenges that don't yet have constituencies or institutional responses: AI in classrooms displacing teachers, voice cloning enabling new categories of fraud, ambient surveillance through consumer wearables, deepfake pornography as a weapon. Overview of international best practices
Curated evidence from global experience on inter-island connectivity, housing, urbanisation, and other policy areas where the Maldives faces challenges that other countries have addressed. What worked, what didn't, and what transfers to a small island developing state context.

Resilience strategy
Resilient and self-reliant
The case for shifting from efficiency-first to resilience-first operating models. Building strategic depth and redundancy in food, fuel, medicine, and critical systems so that supply chain disruptions, climate shocks, and geopolitical shifts don't become existential crises for a country that imports nearly everything.
Read brief →
Climate diplomacy
Treating climate as a crisis
Reframing climate strategy from domestic emissions reduction to diplomatic leverage. The Maldives contributes 0.006% of global emissions – its climate policy should focus on influencing the major emitters whose decisions actually determine whether the country survives, and on ensuring developing countries can leapfrog to clean energy without passing through the fossil fuel stage.
Read brief →
Proactive health
Health policy issues of the future
Proactive health policy for challenges the current system isn't preparing for: pharmaceutical access reform (drug scheduling, monopoly import practices, GLP-1 agonist planning), population-level interventions (salt substitution, disease prevention), and viral cancer prevention strategies. Acting ahead of crisis points rather than reacting to them.

Digital foresight tools

Interactive planning tools for teams and workshops

We developed interactive digital versions of the foresight methodology tools used in planning workshops. Instead of marker-and-flipboard approaches, teams can use these on a shared screen to collaboratively carry out structured planning while still following established foresight principles. Outputs can be saved and combined into a detailed planning report ready at the end of the session.

Influence mapper
Map drivers and their relationships in an interactive matrix. Identify which factors influence which others and trace cascading effects.
